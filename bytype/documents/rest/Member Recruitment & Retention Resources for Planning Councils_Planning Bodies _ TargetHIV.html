Member Recruitment & Retention Resources for Planning Councils/Planning Bodies

Learning collaboratives to support Part A planning councils/planning bodies (PC/PB) in strengthening their member recruitment and retention activities. The group learning sessions were structured for PC/PB teams to participate virtually with the intention of integrating best practices into PC/PB work plans. This effort included peer-to-peer opportunities to share lessons learned, best and promising practices, and strategies to overcome challenges. 

By the end of the learning collaborative, each implementation team: 

  1. Developed a recruitment and retention strategy that prioritizes consumers and people of color.
  2. Assessed and determined which recruitment and retention activities can be integrated into their PC/PB’s work plans.
  3. Identified at least one recruitment or retention activity that can be implemented in the next three months.
Recruitment Strategy – Defining Your Audience

This session focused on reviewing the Ryan White HIV/AIDS Program (RWHAP) legislative requirements for consumer participation, listing the components of a recruitment strategy, and describing the benefits, barriers, and competition for the recruitment audience.

 

Recruitment Messaging and Promotion

This session focused on discussing the 7 C’s of Effective Communication, reviewing the key considerations of the promotion component of the recruitment strategy, and describing at least two PC/PB Recruitment Best Practices.

New Member Engagement, Orientation, and Training

This session focuses on listing essential new member orientation topics, identifying strategies for ongoing training, and describing two examples of committee structures that support retention.

Retention Strategies: Training and Engagement

This session focuses on describing one PC/PB mentorship model and listing the essential elements of a leadership development initiative.
