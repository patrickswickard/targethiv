TAP-in EHE Webinar Series

October 21, 2021
Login to Bookmark

Archive of webinars hosted by the TAP-in: Technical Assistance Provider-innovation network between 2020 and 2025. 

Components

  • Addressing Methamphetamine Use among Black Gay, Bisexual, Same-Gender-Loving Men with HIV
    February 22, 2024
    Discussion of evidence-based interventions for meth users and how to optimize HIV treatment outcomes while reducing harm and stigma.
  • Digital Marketing Strategies to End the HIV Epidemic
    October 30, 2023
    Discussion of the importance of digital marketing to help advance programmatic goals for ending the HIV epidemic.
  • Housing First as a Key Strategy to End the HIV Epidemic
    September 22, 2023
    Review of Housing First, an evidence-based best practice that demonstrates improved health outcomes and care utilization for people experiencing homelessness, including people with HIV.
  • Why Wait? Start Now! What Jurisdictions Can Do to Support Adoption of Rapid Start
    July 19, 2023
    Insights from rapid start sites, determined from an environmental scan, virtual field visits, and jurisdictions participating in the learning community.
  • Coordinating, Communicating, and Collaborating - The 3 Cs for Cluster Detection & Response
    April 4, 2023
    How to create comprehensive Cluster Detection and Response (CDR) programs as part of Ending the HIV Epidemic activities.
  • Integrating Services to Address the Syndemics of HIV, STIs, Substance Use Disorder, and Viral Hepatitis
    March 9, 2023
    Review of how to integrate services using a syndemic approach in order to collectively address HIV, STIs, viral hepatitis, and substance use disorders.
  • How EHE Jurisdictions Can Increase Uptake of Long Acting Injectables for HIV Treatment
    August 24, 2022
    Review of the efficacy of LAIs and implementation activities undertaken by agencies.
  • Imagine: Ending HIV. It's Possible.
    July 5, 2022
    Kickoff of a national campaign to showcase how RWHAP leaders have been innovating to achieve the goal of ending the HIV epidemic.
  • Beyond the Walls: Building Foundation for Jail Linkage Programs
    February 24, 2022
    Effective models and best practices for connection to HIV care for justice-involved individuals and how jurisdictions can overcome common barriers when managing a jail linkage program.
  • Status Neutral Approaches NYC, Texas, and Detroit
    January 27, 2022
    Review of the concept of status-neutral and examples of real-world applications from jurisdiction peers and leaders.
  • Building Partnerships for Data Sharing to Support EHE Plans
    December 9, 2021
    Review of building data partnerships with agencies that manage and control access to data, which can support planning and evaluation of EHE activities.
  • Meth as a Driver of the HIV Epidemic
    September 30, 2021
    Review of the epidemiology of methamphetamine as it relates to HIV, the psychodynamics of meth use, and implications for EHE efforts.
  • At the Intersection of SUD, HIV and COVID-19: Why Trauma Informed Care Matters
    September 9, 2021
    Implementation model to integrate trauma informed care into agency operations.
  • Innovations in the ER: Collaborations among Emergency Departments, TA Providers, & Health Jurisdictions to Identify and Treat HIV, HCV, and Syphilis
    August 10, 2021
    Collaboration among emergency departments and others to implement routine HIV, HCV, and syphilis screening to increase identification and reduce time to treatment.
  • Ending the HIV Epidemic through Innovations in Rapid ART
    July 29, 2021
    Review of innovative Rapid ART strategies in diverse settings.
  • Public Health and Primary Care Partnerships
    June 21, 2021
    Review of the role federally qualified health centers play in the care of people who are uninsured and medically underserved, including people with HIV.
  • Finding and Using the Best Data Sources to Support EHE Strategies
    June 17, 2021
    Tips on linking to the best data sources and helping the EHE team integrate data management into implementation activities.
  • Housing is Healthcare: Best Practices and Common Challenges in HIV Housing
    May 27, 2021
    Overview of HIV housing strategies and program models with a focus on innovation and on leveraging of housing and supportive services.
  • Use of Social Media and Mobile Technology as Essential Tools for EHE
    April 8, 2021
    Strategies to engage hard-to-reach youth and young adults through use of social media and related technologies.
  • Addressing Substance Use Disorders (SUDs) as a Strategy to End the HIV Epidemic
    February 25, 2021
    Review of the impact of SUDs on HIV outcomes and effective evidence-based SUD interventions (Medication Assisted Treatment, Motivational Interviewing, and Contingency Management).
  • Telehealth & HIV: After COVID-19
    February 18, 2021
    Panel discussion of how agencies can adjust telehealth services, often developed rapidly in response to COVID-19, in a post-pandemic environment.
  • Performance Measurement: Using Data to Achieve EHE Goals
    February 3, 2021
    The use of performance measures to help achieve EHE plan goals.
  • Responding to an HIV Cluster or Outbreak: Who do you call? What do you do?
    January 28, 2021
    Model approaches for community partner engagement and coordination to support interventions for the mitigation of HIV outbreaks.
  • Rapid ART: An Essential Strategy to End the HIV Epidemic (Insights from the Field)
    November 19, 2020
    Insights for entities seeking to create or strengthen rapid ART.

Developed by

Technical Assistance Provider Innovation Network (TAP-in)
